Learning Through Monuments.



In the heart of Delhi's bustling throng,
Where history and nature sing a song,
Lies Sunder Nursery, a treasure old,
Where tales of bygone eras unfold.

Set up by the British hand in hand,
On Grand Trunk Road, a storied land,
With CPWD and Aga Khan's grace,
It blooms as a park, a yellow embrace.

Ninety acres of splendor and delight,
Where Mughal monuments greet the light,
Amidst tapestries of flora fair,
A haven for souls seeking fresh air.

A walk within these hallowed grounds,
With nature's symphony all around,
Whispers of the past, stories untold,
In Sunder Nursery, history's mold.

Children play, and elders rest,
In gardens where beauty is expressed,
Cultural events and trails to roam,
A place where hearts find a home.

Humayun's Tomb's legacy bright,
In Lakkarwala Burj, a gleaming light,
Restored with care, its glory shines,
In this park where history aligns.

Rose gardens bloom in vibrant hue,
A tribute to times both old and new,
Where paths of stone and brick unite,
To guide us through this living site.

Sunder Nursery, a blend divine,
Where heritage and nature entwine,
A sanctuary for the soul to find,
In Delhi's heart, a gem designed.

Made by Ambience Public School, Gurugram
